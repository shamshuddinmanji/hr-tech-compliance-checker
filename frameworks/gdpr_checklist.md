# GDPR Compliance Checklist for HR Tech Vendors

> Use this checklist when assessing an HR Technology vendor against the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).
> Score each item and calculate the overall vendor risk score on a 0–5 scale.

---

## Scoring Guide

| Score | Risk Level | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Low Risk | Fully Compliant |
| 4 | Low-Medium | Minor Gaps |
| 3 | Medium | Notable Gaps |
| 2 | High | Significant Gaps |
| 1 | Critical | Major Non-Compliance |
| 0 | Unacceptable | Do Not Proceed |

---

## Section 1 — Lawful Basis for Processing

- [ ] Vendor clearly identifies the lawful basis for processing employee/candidate data
- [ ] Consent mechanisms are documented where applicable
- [ ] Purpose of data processing is explicitly stated
- [ ] Data processing is limited to the stated purpose (purpose limitation)

---

## Section 2 — Data Subject Rights

- [ ] Right to Access — vendor supports data subject access requests (DSAR)
- [ ] Right to Erasure — vendor has a documented data deletion process
- [ ] Right to Portability — data can be exported in a machine-readable format
- [ ] Right to Rectification — individuals can correct inaccurate data
- [ ] Right to Restriction — vendor can restrict processing upon request
- [ ] Response timelines for DSARs are defined (within 30 days per GDPR)

---

## Section 3 — Data Retention & Deletion

- [ ] Data retention periods are clearly defined for each data category
- [ ] Retention policy covers rejected candidates and ex-employees
- [ ] Automated deletion or anonymisation is in place after retention period
- [ ] Retention policy is documented and available upon request

---

## Section 4 — Sub-Processors & Third Parties

- [ ] A list of sub-processors is publicly available or shared upon request
- [ ] Sub-processor agreements include GDPR-compliant data protection clauses
- [ ] Vendor notifies customers of sub-processor changes in advance
- [ ] Sub-processors are located within GDPR-compliant jurisdictions or have safeguards

---

## Section 5 — Cross-Border Data Transfers

- [ ] Vendor identifies where data is stored and processed geographically
- [ ] Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or equivalent are in place for non-EEA transfers
- [ ] Adequacy decisions or Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs) are referenced where applicable

---

## Section 6 — Data Breach Notification

- [ ] Vendor has a documented incident response and breach notification policy
- [ ] Breach notification to customers is within 72 hours of discovery (GDPR requirement)
- [ ] Breach notification process includes severity classification
- [ ] Vendor has a designated Data Protection Officer (DPO) or equivalent contact

---

## Section 7 — Security & Technical Measures

- [ ] Encryption at rest is confirmed
- [ ] Encryption in transit (TLS) is confirmed
- [ ] Access controls and role-based permissions are in place
- [ ] Regular security audits or penetration testing are conducted
- [ ] Vendor holds relevant security certifications (ISO 27001, SOC 2, etc.)

---

## Section 8 — Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

- [ ] Vendor provides a GDPR-compliant Data Processing Agreement
- [ ] DPA clearly defines roles (Controller vs Processor)
- [ ] DPA includes data protection obligations for sub-processors
- [ ] DPA is signed and up to date
