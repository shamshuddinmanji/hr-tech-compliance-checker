# SOC 2 Compliance Checklist for HR Tech Vendors

> Use this checklist when assessing an HR Technology vendor against the SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria.
> Score each item and calculate the overall vendor risk score on a 0–5 scale.

---

## Scoring Guide

| Score | Risk Level | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Low Risk | Fully Compliant |
| 4 | Low-Medium | Minor Gaps |
| 3 | Medium | Notable Gaps |
| 2 | High | Significant Gaps |
| 1 | Critical | Major Non-Compliance |
| 0 | Unacceptable | Do Not Proceed |

---

## Section 1 — Security (Common Criteria)

- [ ] Vendor has a formal information security policy in place
- [ ] Logical access controls are implemented (role-based access, least privilege)
- [ ] Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is enforced for system access
- [ ] Encryption at rest and in transit is confirmed
- [ ] Vulnerability management and patch management processes are documented
- [ ] Security awareness training is conducted for employees
- [ ] Penetration testing is performed at least annually
- [ ] Vendor holds a current SOC 2 Type II report (preferred over Type I)

---

## Section 2 — Availability

- [ ] System uptime SLAs are clearly defined (e.g. 99.9% availability)
- [ ] Disaster recovery (DR) plan is documented and tested
- [ ] Business continuity plan (BCP) is in place
- [ ] Incident response procedures are defined with escalation paths
- [ ] Planned maintenance windows are communicated to customers in advance

---

## Section 3 — Confidentiality

- [ ] Confidential data is identified and classified
- [ ] Access to confidential data is restricted on a need-to-know basis
- [ ] Non-disclosure agreements (NDAs) are in place with employees and contractors
- [ ] Confidential data is securely disposed of when no longer needed
- [ ] Data masking or anonymisation is applied where appropriate

---

## Section 4 — Processing Integrity

- [ ] Data processing is complete, accurate, and timely
- [ ] Input and output validation controls are in place
- [ ] Error handling and exception reporting processes are documented
- [ ] Data processing errors are logged and reviewed

---

## Section 5 — Privacy

- [ ] Privacy policy is publicly available and up to date
- [ ] Personal data collection is limited to what is necessary (data minimisation)
- [ ] Individuals are notified about data collection and its purpose
- [ ] Opt-out or consent withdrawal mechanisms are available
- [ ] Privacy incidents are tracked and reported

---

## Section 6 — Vendor Risk & Third Party Management

- [ ] Vendor conducts due diligence on its own sub-processors and third parties
- [ ] Third party contracts include security and confidentiality obligations
- [ ] Vendor monitors third party compliance on an ongoing basis

---

## Section 7 — Audit & Reporting

- [ ] SOC 2 Type II report is available and shared upon request
- [ ] Report covers the relevant audit period (minimum 6 months)
- [ ] No qualified opinions or exceptions in the most recent SOC 2 report
- [ ] Vendor is willing to complete customer security questionnaires
